More information about paid and unpaid medical bills.

More information for you because you have paid medical bills.

Paid medical bills may be used as a deduction in determining eligibility for health care coverage.Paid medical bills may also be used as a deduction in determining eligibility for SNAP (formerly known as Food Stamps) when the household adds an elderly or disabled member.Some examples of paid medical bills are doctor bills, hospital bills, prescription costs, and health insurance premium bills. The following medical bills may be used as a deduction for health care coverage:

  • Medical bills paid in the month the applicant(s) applied.
  • Medical bills the applicant(s) paid up to three months before applying may be used as a deduction depending on the Medical Assistance Program being reviewed.

    • More information for you because you have unpaid medical bills.

    Unpaid medical bills may be used as a deduction in determining eligibility for health care coverage. If the applicant(s) received medical services in the past 3 months that are not paid yet, we may be able to pay them for the applicant(s). If we cannot pay the unpaid medical bills, we may use them as a deduction in determining the eligibility for health care coverage. The applicant(s) will be asked to show copies of these bills and proof of income for these months
