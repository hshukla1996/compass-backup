If you receive cash assistance, you are required to cooperate by establishing paternity and applying for child support for your children. If you do not, your cash grant may be reduced unless there is 'good cause', like a domestic violence situation. If there is 'good cause', you may not have to establish paternity or apply for child support right away. If you have questions about support, call the state Bureau of Child Support Enforcement toll free at 1-800-932-0211.

When you accept cash assistance, you must allow the state to collect the support. If support is collected, the support is used to pay the state for the cash assistance paid to your family. If support is more than the cash assistance paid to your family, the rest of the support is given back to you.
