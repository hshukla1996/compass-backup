Is everyone in the household age 60 or older or permanently disabled?

A permanent disability is any of these:

  • Receiving Supplemental Security Income (SSI) because of blindness or disability
  • Receiving disability benefits from Social Security Administration (SSA)
  • A veteran who is considered disabled by the Veterans Administration (VA)
  • A veteran considered by the Veterans Administration (VA) to be in need of aid and attendance or permanently housebound
  • Receiving Railroad Retirement disability benefits and is eligible for Medicare
  • Certified as disabled by the state Medical Review Team
  • Certified by a doctor as permanently disabled and unable to work (proof will be required)

Do all household members age 60 or older who are permanently disabled buy and make their food apart from other household members who are not age 60 or older or permanently disabled?

Do any household members age 60 or older or who are permanently disabled receive earnings from work?

By earnings from work, we mean any job a household member has and is making money at, including self-employment.
